## Mission Briefing

<p class="flavor">
Something BAD is about to happen on this planet. You're not exactly sure WHAT, and you're not exactly sure WHEN, but look around you - with this many enemies nearby, something bad is bound to happen. Grab the objective, kill anyone who tries to take it from you, and wait for the end.</p>

## Setup and Deployment

1. Setup terrain as you usually would, but place your tallest ruin in the center of the board. The single objective marker should go on the top floor, above the map's centerpoint.
2. Before rolling Attacker and Defender, determine the deployment teams. Each player rolls d6, re-rolling any ties. Once everyone has a unique roll, the player with the highest roll is paired with the player with the lowest roll, and the two players in the middle are paired.
3. Roll Attacker and Defender as a team, with the winning team chosing to either deploy second or choose their deployment zone.
4. Proceed through deployment, alternating sides after both teammates have placed a unit. Teammates must share their deployment zone, may deploy within engagement range of their teammate, and should generally 
5. Roll for first turn, with each player rolling d6. Again, reroll all ties until each player has a unique roll. Turn order in the Battle Round is as follows:
    1. The player with the highest roll
    2. Their teammate
    3. The player with the highest roll on the other team
    4. Their teammate

## Mission Rules

- No Backstabbing (YET!): 
    - During the first Battle Round, you may not attack your teammate in any way. This means no targeting them with attacks, no hitting them with psychic powers EVEN IF that power doesn't do damage, etc. Powers like Smite that target the closest enemy unit will ignore your teammate and instead hit the nearest non-teammate unit. The only exception here would be a) explosions, or b) certain powers that do damage to ALL units in range (essentially, your teammate is not considered to be an ENEMY in the first Battle Round). 
    - During the second Battle Round, you may attack your teammate. However, either the attacker or the target must be outside your deployment zone (no castling up to backstab your teammate Turn 2)
    
- Unstable Objective: This mission lasts a variable number of rounds. The first two turns are guaranteed. Starting at the end of the second Battle Round (and at the end of any subsequent Battle Rounds), roll d6 and consult the chart below. If you roll the target number, the game continues. Otherwise, the game ends immediately - total up the score and determine the winner.

## Mission Objectives

- King of the Hill: Control the objective at the end of the game to score 3 points. If the objective is contested, each player contesting the objective gains 1 point instead.
- Linebreaker: Score 1 point if you have a unit fully within the opponents deployment zone. Score 2 points instead if you have three units fully within.
- Slay the Warlord: Score 1 point if you slay an enemy warlord this game. Score 2 points instead if you slay three warlords.
